# Toy Model 5G-N - Relational Metric Generation Law Roadmap

## Why 5G-N is needed

Toy Models 5G-L and 5G-M showed that the residual object should be treated as a metric / connection distortion:

`chi(R) = ln[g_obs(R) / g_bar(R)]`

This object handles both positive and negative departures from the baryonic baseline. It is better aligned with the idea that the hidden structure is a relational metric/connection, not merely a positive dark-sector acceleration.

However, 5G-L/M used statistical reconstruction. The next step must replace statistical reconstruction with a physics-style generation law.

## 5G-N purpose

5G-N should ask:

> Can chi(R) be generated from baryonic organization, topology, and boundary conditions by one universal relational metric law?

## Candidate mathematical direction

The model should attempt to build a generation equation of the form:

```text
chi(R) = G_rel[baryonic organization, topology, boundary conditions]
```

where the inputs may include:

- local baryonic acceleration;
- radial baryonic gradients;
- effective enclosed baryonic mass proxy;
- total knot mass proxy;
- gas fraction / disk dominance proxy;
- compactness / surface-density proxy;
- relational connectivity features;
- boundary insufficiency features.

## Guardrails

5G-N must not introduce:

- dark halo parameters;
- galaxy-specific correction terms;
- per-galaxy fitted metric laws;
- post-hoc removal of failed galaxies;
- claims of validation before external reproduction.

## Success conditions

5G-N is successful at protocol level if it defines one frozen metric-generation law and tests it on held-out galaxies.

It is successful at model level only if that frozen law predicts chi(R) across a broad SPARC sample better than the 5G-M statistical placeholder and without hidden tuning.

## Failure conditions

5G-N fails if:

- each galaxy requires its own generation law;
- systematic galaxy classes remain badly wrong;
- additional hidden parameters are needed to rescue failures;
- the law cannot outperform simpler empirical baselines.

## Correct public wording

Allowed:

> 5G-N is the next attempt to move from empirical metric reconstruction to a relational metric generation law.

Not allowed:

> 5G-N will prove the 6D framework or solve dark matter.
