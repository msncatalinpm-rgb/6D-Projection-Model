# Core Proposal

The 6D Projection Model proposes that aspects of standard quantum mechanics may admit geometric reinterpretation as projection phenomena arising from a deeper relational-temporal structure.

The framework does NOT replace quantum mechanics or general relativity.

Instead, it attempts to reinterpret:

- quantum probability,
- interference,
- and nonlocal correlation

through projection geometry and hidden relational structure.

---

# Minimal Conceptual Core

## Projection Geometry

The framework interprets the Born rule:

$$
P(x)=|\langle x|\Psi\rangle|^2
$$

as a geometric projection measure rather than as a primitive probabilistic axiom.

---

## Symmetry Stabilization

Observable spacetime is treated as a symmetry-stabilized projection preserving effective Lorentz consistency within the classical sector.

$$
SO(3,3) \rightarrow SO(3,1)
$$

---

## Hidden Relational Accessibility

Nonlocal quantum correlations are interpreted as manifestations of hidden relational proximity within the relational-temporal sector rather than as observable superluminal transport.

---

## Open Frontier

The framework tentatively introduces a split-metric structure only as an exploratory mathematical scaffold.

The framework remains mathematically incomplete and experimentally unresolved.

---

# Important Scope Limitations

The framework:

- does NOT claim that quantum mechanics is wrong,
- does NOT claim experimental validation,
- does NOT propose faster-than-light signaling,
- does NOT propose macroscopic time travel,
- and is NOT presented as a completed unified theory.

The project is intended as an open conceptual and mathematical investigation.
